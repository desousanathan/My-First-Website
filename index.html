What is F1

One of the most adrenaline-filled sport, Formula 1 (also known as F1) is the highest class of international motorsport which is an open-wheel single-seater category of racing, starting in March and ending in November in which 10 different teams compete in multiple Grand Prix race, in which two different drivers per team compete to get a higher position in the race. These races span over 3 continents over 26 different races every calendar

Structure of Formula 1

Formula 1 is a sport that is biased on cars racing around different circuits for nearly a year. In there types of races, 20 drivers go around the track in which they are racing on for about 52 times for a race. These races last about 2 hours per race and are very physically demanding on the drivers.

There races consist of:

  • An average of 52 laps per race
  • A pit stop or 2 per race
  • Action packed racing involving overtakes as well as crashes
